# Giving commands and making polite requests

The Kyrgyz verb stem *is* the plain command form — there's no separate
imperative ending to learn. *сүйлө* means both "to speak" and "Speak!"; *оку*
is both "to read" and "Read!"; *бер* is both "to give" and "Give!"

**Negation** adds -ба/-бе/-бо/-бө (or -па/-пе/-по/-пө after a voiceless
sound) to the stem: барба "don't go", ичпе "don't drink".

**Addressing more than one person** adds -гыла/-гиле/-гула/-гүлө to the
stem: баргыла "go!" (to several people), келгиле "come!" (to several
people).

**Politeness** — Kyrgyz has no word for "please". Instead, the particle
-чы/-чу/-чи/-чү is added to a stem (or to its polite -ыңыз form) to
soften a command into a request: аччы "open, please", *кайталап
коюңузчу* "repeat, please" (built on the polite stem коюңуз).
