# Naming occupations, and saying "not"

The suffix **-чы/-чу/-чи/-чү** turns a noun or verb stem into "the person who
does [it]": жумуш "work" → жумушчу "worker"; дүкөн "shop" → *дүкөнчү*
"shopkeeper"; оку- "to study" → *окуучу* "student, pupil". This is the same
suffix used for polite requests (see the notes on сабак 7) — context tells
them apart.

**Эмес** negates a noun, adjective, or adverb — the equivalent of English
"not": *бала* "child" → бала эмес "not a child"; *жакшы* "good" → жакшы
эмес "not good". In conversation it answers a yes/no question about
identity:

— Бул досуңбу? — Жок, досум эмес, байкем.
*"Is this your friend?" — "No, not my friend, my older brother."*

(Эмес negates being-a-thing; it doesn't negate verbs — verb negation uses the
-ба/-бе/-бо/-бө suffix from сабак 7 instead.)
