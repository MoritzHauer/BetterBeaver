# Choosing the right greeting

Kyrgyz greetings are chosen by the age and status of the person you address,
and most come in fixed pairs — one side opens, the other side has a set reply.

- *Салам!* — between friends and people your own age. Reply: Салам!
- *Саламатсызбы?* — the polite all-purpose greeting for elders, teachers,
  and strangers, usable at any time of day. An elder replies Саламатчылык!;
  between two unfamiliar equals the reply just repeats Саламатсызбы?
- *Ассалом алейкум!* — traditionally for an elderly or respected man
  (an аксакал). Reply: Алейкум ассалом!

The younger person greets first. After the greeting, ask how the other is
doing: *Иштер кандай?* with peers, or the polite *Иштериңиз кандай?*
with anyone you address as сиз. Answer *Рахмат, жакшы* or *Жаман эмес*.

The same politeness split runs through the whole language: *сен* is the
informal "you" for friends and children, *сиз* the polite "you" for
everyone else. A Kyrgyz proverb pins it: *«Сиз» деген сылык сөз, «сен» деген
сенек сөз* — "Siz is a polite word, sen is a blunt word." When unsure, use сиз.
