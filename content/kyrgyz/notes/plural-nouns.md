# Making nouns plural

Kyrgyz adds a plural suffix directly to a noun, before any possessive or case
ending. Which vowel it uses follows the noun's own vowels (vowel harmony), and
which consonant follows the sound before it:

- After a vowel or **й**, **р** → -лар, -лер, -лор, -лөр
- After a voiced consonant → -дар, -дер, -дор, -дөр
- After a voiceless consonant → -тар, -тер, -тор, -төр

Examples: *бала* "child" → балдар "children"; *кыз* "girl" → кыздар
"girls"; китеп "book" → китептер "books"; *мугалим* "teacher" → мугалимдер
"teachers".

The same suffix attaches to pronouns too — that's how *ал* "he/she/it"
becomes *алар* "they", and *сиз* "you (polite)" becomes сиздер "you all
(polite)".

Asking "who/what are they?" uses the plural question words **Кимдер?** and
**Эмнелер?**, mirroring the plural forms of *ким?* and *эмне?*.
