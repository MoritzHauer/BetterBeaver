# The genitive case: whose?

The genitive case marks who or what something belongs to, answering
**Кимдин?** "whose?" (for people) or **Эмненин?** "of what?" (for things).
Like the plural suffix, its vowel and consonant follow the noun it attaches to:

- After a vowel → *-нын, -нин, -нун, -нүн*
- After a voiced consonant → *-дын, -дин, -дун, -дүн*
- After a voiceless consonant → *-тын, -тин, -тун, -түн*

Examples: *бала* "child" → *баланын* "the child's"; *Асан* → *Асандын*
"Asan's"; *китеп* "book" → *китептин* "the book's"; *мектеп* "school" →
*мектептин* "the school's".

A genitive noun is normally followed by the thing it possesses, which itself
takes a possessive ending: *Асандын комузу* "Asan's komuz" (literally "Asan's
komuz-his"). This is the same double-marking possessive phrases always use.
